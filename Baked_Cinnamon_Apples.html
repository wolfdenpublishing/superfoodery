Baked Cinnamon Apples

Whole apples stuffed with a walnut-oat filling spiced with cinnamon, ginger, turmeric, and optional habanero, baked until tender and served with a dollop of Greek yogurt. A warming, naturally sweet dessert packed with omega-3s, beta-glucan fiber, and anti-inflammatory compounds.

Ingredients

Apples

  • 4 medium apples (Jazz or Granny Smith)
  • Foil cupcake liners or coconut oil for muffin tin

Filling

  • 1/3 cup walnuts, chopped
  • 3 tbsp rolled oats
  • 4 tsp honey
  • 3/4 tsp Ceylon cinnamon
  • 3/4 tsp fresh ginger, finely grated
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric
  • Pinch of black pepper
  • Pinch of habanero powder (optional)

Topping

  • 1/2 cup plain full-fat Greek yogurt (2 tbsp per apple)

Instructions

Prepare the Apples
  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Line 4 muffin tin cups with foil liners, or lightly coat with coconut oil.
  2. Core apples from the top, leaving about 1/2 inch at the bottom intact. Widen the cavity slightly with a spoon if needed.
Make the Filling and Bake
  1. Mix walnuts, oats, cinnamon, ginger, turmeric, black pepper, and habanero powder in a small bowl. Drizzle in honey and stir to combine.
  2. Stuff filling into each apple, pressing gently. Mound any extra on top.
  3. Place apples in muffin tin cups. Bake 30-35 minutes until apples are tender when pierced with a knife.
Serve
  1. Serve warm with a dollop of Greek yogurt (2 tbsp per apple).

Notes

  • OMAD Snack: At ~250 calories, this pairs well as an OMAD snack alongside any of the main dish recipes.
  • Apple Varieties: Jazz and Granny Smith hold their shape well when baked. Avoid Red Delicious — they go mealy with heat.
  • Don't Peel: The skin holds the apple together during baking, and most of the quercetin and fiber are concentrated in and just under the skin.
  • Muffin Tin: Use foil cupcake liners for easy cleanup, or lightly coat the cups with coconut oil. Avoid paper liners — the juices soak through and stick.
  • Turmeric: Subtle at this amount — you won't taste it, but paired with black pepper and the fat from the walnuts, you get the absorption benefit.
  • Storage: Refrigerate up to 4 days. Reheat in toaster oven to maintain texture. Add yogurt after reheating.
  • Scaling: Easily doubled to fill a standard 6- or 12-cup muffin tin.
