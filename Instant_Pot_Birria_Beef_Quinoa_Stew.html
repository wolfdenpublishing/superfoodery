Instant Pot Birria Beef & Quinoa Stew

A bold, spicy birria-style beef stew made with grass-fed rib steaks, homemade dried chili paste, and protein-rich quinoa. The toasted chili paste creates layers of complex, smoky heat while the beef becomes fall-apart tender. Perfect for meal prep - the flavors deepen overnight and it reheats beautifully.

Ingredients

For the Beef

  • 4 lb grass-fed beef rib steaks
  • 6 cups beef stock, divided

For the Chili Paste

  • 3 dried guajillo chilies
  • 3 dried jalapeño chilies
  • 3 dried cayenne or banana peppers

For the Stew

  • 1 cup quinoa, dry
  • 1 lb carrots, sliced
  • 1 large yellow onion, diced
  • 6 sweet peppers (mixed colors), diced
  • 1 can (6 oz) tomato paste

Seasonings

  • 1 tbsp cumin
  • 1 tsp oregano
  • 1/2 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp habañero powder
  • 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Pressure Cook the Beef
  1. Place beef rib steaks in Instant Pot with 2 cups beef stock.
  2. Pressure cook on high for 90 minutes with natural release.
  3. Remove steaks, separate meat from bones, and shred the beef. Set aside.
Make the Chili Paste
  1. While beef cooks, remove stems and seeds from dried chilies.
  2. Toast chilies in a dry pan over medium heat for 30 seconds per side until fragrant. Do not blacken.
  3. Place toasted chilies in a bowl and cover with hot water. Soak for 15-20 minutes until soft.
  4. Transfer chilies to a blender with enough soaking liquid to blend into a smooth paste. Reserve remaining soaking liquid.
Build the Stew
  1. To the cooking liquid in the Instant Pot, add quinoa, carrots, onion, sweet peppers, tomato paste, cumin, oregano, garlic powder, and habañero powder.
  2. Pressure cook on high for 2 minutes with quick release.
  3. Stir in the shredded beef and chili paste.
  4. Add remaining beef stock (about 4 cups) plus reserved chili soaking liquid until desired consistency is reached.
  5. Stir in apple cider vinegar. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Let stand 5 minutes to allow flavors to meld before serving or portioning for meal prep.

Notes

Meal Prep: This stew is ideal for meal prep. Portion into individual containers and refrigerate for up to 5 days or freeze for up to 3 months. The flavors intensify overnight. Reheat in microwave until hot throughout.

Chili Heat: The heat level depends on your dried chili mix. Adjust habañero powder up or down to taste. The toasting step is optional but adds significant depth of flavor.

For Fat Loss Phase: At 613 calories per serving with 43g protein, this works well as a main meal during fat loss phase, leaving room for a small snack or side of vegetables.

Superfood Benefits: Grass-fed beef provides quality protein and omega-3s. Quinoa adds complete plant protein and fiber. Alliums (onion, garlic) and peppers deliver antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds.
