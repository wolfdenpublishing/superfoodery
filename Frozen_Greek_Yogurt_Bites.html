Frozen Greek Yogurt Bites

Creamy Greek yogurt mixed with your choice of fresh fruit, walnuts, and dark chocolate, frozen in silicone molds for perfectly portioned treats. A protein-rich, probiotic-packed dessert that satisfies sweet cravings while delivering superfood nutrition.

Ingredients

Base

  • 2 cups plain Greek yogurt (whole milk)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp honey (for mixing into base)

Mix-ins

  • 1/2 cup fruit of choice, diced small:
    • Fresh peaches, OR
    • Strawberries, OR
    • Blueberries (leave whole)
  • 3 tbsp dark chocolate chips (mini preferred)
  • 1/4 cup walnuts, roughly chopped

Topping

  • Reserved fruit, chocolate chips, and walnut pieces
  • 1 tbsp honey for drizzling

Instructions

Mix the Base
  1. In a medium bowl, stir together Greek yogurt, vanilla extract, and 1 tablespoon honey until smooth and well combined.
Add Mix-ins
  1. If using peaches or strawberries, dice into approximately 1/4-inch pieces. Blueberries can be left whole.
  2. Reserve a small handful of fruit, chocolate chips, and walnut pieces for topping.
  3. Gently fold the remaining fruit, chocolate chips, and chopped walnuts into the yogurt mixture.
Fill and Decorate
  1. Spoon the mixture into silicone molds, filling to just below the rim. For 3-inch rounds, this makes approximately 6 pieces.
  2. Tap molds gently on the counter to settle the mixture and remove air bubbles.
  3. Press reserved fruit pieces, chocolate chips, and walnut pieces into the surface of each portion.
  4. Lightly drizzle the remaining 1 tablespoon honey over the tops.
Freeze and Store
  1. Place molds on a flat surface in the freezer. Freeze until completely solid, at least 3-4 hours or overnight.
  2. Once frozen, pop out of molds and transfer to a freezer-safe container or bag. Keep frozen until ready to eat.

Notes

  • Texture preference: Straight from the freezer, these are quite firm and satisfying to crunch. Let sit 2-3 minutes for a softer, creamier texture.
  • Mold options: Use 3" rounds for a substantial dessert (~170 cal), or 1" squares for bite-sized snacking (~45-50 cal each).
  • Yogurt swap: Using 2% Greek yogurt instead of whole milk reduces the batch total by about 80-100 calories.
  • Storage: Keeps frozen for up to 2 weeks. Store in an airtight container to prevent freezer burn.
  • Superfood benefits: Greek yogurt provides probiotics for gut health and high-quality protein. Walnuts deliver omega-3 fatty acids. Dark chocolate offers flavanols and antioxidants.
