Peach Chia Pudding

A creamy, satisfying chia pudding featuring fresh peaches. Chia seeds deliver omega-3s and an impressive 12g of fiber per serving, while peaches add vitamin C, beta-carotene, and natural sweetness. Perfect as an OMAD snack during fat loss phase.

Ingredients

Base Pudding

  • 3 tbsp chia seeds
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened almond milk (or coconut milk)
  • 1 medium ripe peach, diced (or 1/2 cup frozen peaches, thawed)
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/8 tsp cinnamon (Ceylon preferred)
  • 1 tsp honey or maple syrup (optional, to taste)

Optional Add-ins

  • 1 tbsp hemp seeds (+55 cal, +3g protein)
  • 1/4 cup Greek yogurt (+75 cal, +5g protein)
  • 1 oz walnuts or pecans (+185-195 cal)

Instructions

Method 1: Layered (Simpler)
  1. In a jar or container, whisk together chia seeds, almond milk, vanilla extract, and cinnamon until well combined.
  2. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight. Stir once after 30 minutes to prevent clumping.
  3. When ready to serve, dice the peach and layer on top of the set pudding. Drizzle with honey if desired.
Method 2: Blended (Creamier)
  1. Blend half the peach with the almond milk until smooth.
  2. Pour the peach milk into a jar, then whisk in chia seeds, vanilla, and cinnamon.
  3. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight, stirring once after 30 minutes.
  4. Top with remaining diced peach and optional honey before serving.

Fat Loss Phase (1,500 cal): The base recipe at 235 calories fits perfectly as an OMAD snack, leaving ~1,265 calories for your main meal.

Meal Prep: Make 3-4 jars at once for the week. The chia base keeps 4-5 days refrigerated. Add fresh fruit just before eating for best texture.

Fruit Swaps: Substitute strawberries or wild blueberries for similar calories. Frozen wild blueberries work beautifully in the blended method.

Superfood Benefits: Chia seeds provide omega-3 ALA, fiber for gut health and satiety, plus plant-based protein. Peaches contribute vitamin C, potassium, and polyphenols.
