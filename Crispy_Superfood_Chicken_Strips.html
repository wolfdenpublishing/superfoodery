Crispy Superfood Chicken Strips & Sweet Potato Fries

A superfood reimagining of classic chicken strips and fries. Almond flour and flaxseed coating delivers crunch while adding omega-3s and fiber. Sweet potato fries provide beta-carotene, and the Greek yogurt dipping sauce adds protein and probiotics.

Instructions

Prepare the Coating
  1. In a shallow bowl, mix almond flour, ground flaxseed, smoked paprika, turmeric, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne, black pepper, and salt until well combined.
  2. Beat the egg in a separate shallow bowl.
Coat the Chicken
  1. Pat chicken strips dry with paper towels.
  2. Dip each strip in beaten egg, allowing excess to drip off.
  3. Press into the coating mixture, turning to coat all sides evenly.
  4. Place coated strips on a plate or wire rack while preparing remaining pieces.
Prepare the Sweet Potato Fries
  1. Toss sweet potato wedges with avocado oil, smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper until evenly coated.
Air Fryer Method
  1. Air fry sweet potato fries at 400¬∞F for 15-18 minutes, shaking halfway through.
  2. Air fry chicken strips at 400¬∞F for 10-12 minutes, flipping halfway, until golden and cooked through (internal temp 165¬∞F).
Oven Method (Batch Cooking)
  1. Preheat oven to 425¬∞F. Place wire racks on two sheet pans.
  2. Arrange sweet potato fries on the lower rack. Bake for 25-30 minutes total, flipping halfway.
  3. Add chicken strips to the upper rack about 10 minutes after the fries. Bake 18-22 minutes, flipping halfway.
  4. Optional: Broil 2-3 minutes at the end for extra crispiness (watch carefully).
Make the Dipping Sauce
  1. Whisk together Greek yogurt, Dijon mustard, lemon juice, turmeric, and black pepper. Add hot sauce if desired.
Serve
  1. Serve chicken strips and fries immediately with dipping sauce on the side.
